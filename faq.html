Frequently Asked Questions


We understand that embarking on having a website designed and developed can be sometimes daunting. - We crafted this FAQ page to help you ease into things, and answer some of the more common questions we get asked. If you don’t find what you’re looking for here, please contact us and we’ll be happy to answer any questions you may have.

What is a responsive website?

Explained very simply, a responsive website is one that adapts to the screen size of the device where the website is being served. So when the page resizes, the layout adjusts according to the fit of the screen.

Why do I need to have a responsive website?

Besides desktops, web browsing on tablets and mobile phones is on the rise. Therefore, if you don’t have a responsive, mobile-friendly website in the technically advanced environment today, you risk losing valuable traffic, branding and business.

How long will it take to launch my responsive website after the initial meeting?

The time it takes to produce a website depends on the size and complexity of your site, although the time frames vary, it typically takes an average 3-4 weeks from beginning to end to create a responsive website

Can I convert my existing website design into a responsive design

Yes, of course! We at Nubrink Design are here to not only create a responsive website from scratch, but also convert existing ones to become mobile-friendly.

How much does a responsive website cost?

This can vary depending on things such as how big the website is (number of pages) and the features and functionality that is included in the website. Our typical website projects start from around $4000.

Again, the cost is a function of how extensive your particular project is. We can assure you, however, of the most reasonable rates for the quality you’ll get. Contact us to discuss your individual project and the costs involved.

What is Search Engine Optimisation (SEO)?

Search Engine Optimisation is all about getting your website higher up in the search results on search engines such as Google. The higher up the results you are the more traffic (and customers) you’re likely to get.
